As seen by - moving from assumptions to intentions for happiness and profit

Abstract: If devops is a cultural and professional movement aiming at communication and silos take-down, where is the discussion about culture and communication? Tools can definitely help, a convergence on things like infrastructure as code, testing and continuous integration are helping to build a common vocabulary on which dialog can be easier, but they are no substitute for communication. 'As seen by' questions how departments see each other and how that influences the tensions between them, and offers a way out by focusing on intentions.

Etsy has blogged about optimizing for developer happiness http://codeascraft.etsy.com/2011/06/06/optimizing-for-developer-happiness/ speaking to the value this brings to their company; 'As seen by' discusses the same topic with a focus on communication and how to optimize it for engineering happiness while looking at common communication problems and their solutions and how teams can benefit from it.

Proposer:Spike Morelli

Spike walked the unix way and read the unix koans, meditating on infrastructures, scalability and monitoring for many years. Intrigued by development he dabbled as a python dev until he decided to go solo and dedicate himself 100% to product development with his startup. Tracing back his steps he ended up into management where he got to finally appreciate the 'people problem'. Fascinated by communication and impact of happy teams he began his quest to better understand what drives people and how to build effective teams.
